What is AEIS/TAPR?

AEIS is the program of the Texas Education Agency that stands for Academic Excellence Indicator. It has changed its name to the Texas Academic Performance Reports in the past 2 years. (TAPR)

The data is available on their site from the past 20 years. We have taken just about all of the data from that site, and tried to put it in a format that's a little easier to gather and work with.

You can download whatever data you're looking for at our Bulk download pageor you can browse by the campus level on our campus page or at the district and state level on our district page.

There are 254 Counties in Texas with 8,254 schools and 1257 School Districts. Our state is made up of 4.5 million students and 334,354 teachers so there's a lot of data.

Where can I find the data?

The state opens up the data on their website here.

The data is aggregated at 4 levels:

  • Campus
  • District
  • Region
  • State

Each campus is given a campus number that is 9 digits long. The first 3 digits are the county, second three are the district, and then the campus. For example, 057905225:

  • 057 - Dallas County
  • 905 - Dallas ISD
  • 225 - Woodrow Wilson High School
